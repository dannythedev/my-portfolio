Algorithmic Highlights

Calculating Cell screen positions of \(N\) Cells in \(O(1)\) Runtime instead of \(O(N)\).

Introduction

This function determines if the player's mouse cursor is currently positioned over a cell. If it is, then it updates the current coordinates as the current cell's indices.

Mathematical Evaluation

Naive Approach:
  • Time Complexity: \(O(n^2)\) due to iterating over all grid cells to check for collisions with the mouse cursor.
Optimized Approach:
  • Utilizes a bounding box check to quickly determine if the mouse cursor is within the grid area.
  • Computes the mouse cell location directly using simple arithmetic operations. In this case, Let:
    • \( S_x^{\text{max}} \), \( S_y^{\text{max}} \): Dimensions of the screen in pixels.
    • \( M_x \), \( M_y \): Current mouse position on the game's screen in pixels.
    • \( w \), \( h \): Number of cells in each row and column.
    • \( C \): Size of each cell in pixels.
    • \( S \): Constant spacing between adjacent cells, adjusted based on the scale of the cells in pixels.
    • \( D \): Total dimension of each cell, defined as \( C + S \).
    • \( G_x^{\text{min}} \), \( G_y^{\text{min}} \): Starting x and y-coordinates of the grid in pixels, computed as \( \left\lfloor \frac{{S_x^{\text{max}} - (w \times D) }}{{2}} \right\rfloor\) and \( \left\lfloor \frac{{S_y^{\text{max}} - (h \times D) }}{{2}} \right\rfloor\) respectively.
    • \( G_x^{\text{max}} \), \( G_y^{\text{max}} \): Ending x and y-coordinates of the grid in pixels, computed as \( G_{x}^{\text{min}} + w \times D \) and \( G_{y}^{\text{min}} + h \times D \) respectively.
    • \( \text{col} \), \( \text{row} \): Row and column indices of the cell that the mouse is hovering over, measured in cell indices.

    So that, if \( G_x \leq M_x < G_x^{\text{max}} \) and \( G_y \leq M_y < G_y^{\text{max}} \) are within bounds, then:

    • \( \text{col} = \left\lfloor \frac{{M_x - G_x}}{{D}} \right\rfloor \)
    • \( \text{row} = \left\lfloor \frac{{M_y - G_y}}{{D}} \right\rfloor \)
  • Time Complexity: \(O(1)\), as the computation time remains constant regardless of grid size.

Calculating Mouse Precision Movement \((CPM)\).

Introduction

This mechanic is responsible for tracking and evaluating the performance of the mouse movements made by the player during gameplay.

Mathematical Evaluation

  • Let \( (x_{\text{prev}}, y_{\text{prev}}) \) be the mouse position in the previous cell reveal, and \( (x_{\text{curr}}, y_{\text{curr}}) \) be the mouse position in the current cell reveal.
  • Let \( d_{\text{mouse}} \) be the total distance traveled by the mouse between cell reveals.
  • Let \( d_{\text{min}} \) be the minimum distance between the current and previous mouse positions. \( d_{\text{min}} = \sqrt{(x_{\text{curr}} - x_{\text{prev}})^2 + (y_{\text{curr}} - y_{\text{prev}})^2} \)
  • The current accuracy is calculated as a percentage of \( d_{\text{min}} \) relative to the total distance traveled: \( \text{current_accuracy} = \frac{d_{\text{min}}}{d_{\text{mouse}}} \times 100 \).
  • Accumulate the sum of all accuracies for every set of two consecutive clicks, and divide them by their amount, showing the mean of all accuracies in real-time.

Calculating Clicks Per Second \((CPS)\)

Introduction

This mechanic tracks and evaluates the user's pace by measuring the rate at which cells are successfully revealed per second during gameplay.

Mathematical Evaluation

  • Let \( T \) be the total time elapsed during gameplay, measured in seconds.
  • Let \( C \) be the total number of cells successfully revealed during the time period \( T \).
  • The Clicks Per Second (\( CPS \)) is calculated as the ratio of the total number of cells revealed (\( C \)) to the total time elapsed (\( T \)): \[ CPS = \frac{C}{T} \]
